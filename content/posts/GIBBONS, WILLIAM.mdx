<p><strong>JUEL ID/KEY:</strong>&nbsp;<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=44626" title="P44626">P44626</a></p><p>William Gibbons, male,&nbsp;a prominent individual in the Charlottesville community. Spouse of <a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/400" title="GIBBONS, ISABELLA">Isabella Gibbons</a>&nbsp;(<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=44625" title="P44625">P44625</a>). William was employed by Prof. William McGuffey (<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=43668" title="P43668">P43668</a>), who lived in Pavilion IX (<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_place?place=8470" target="_blank" title="PL8470">PL8470</a>) from 1845 to 1873, and was owned by Henry Howard (<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=43670" title="P43670">P43670</a>).</p><p>Details about her later life (marriage, residence, death) are not as clear. William Gibbons was the Minister of the First Baptist Church, Charlottesville after 1863; in 1868, he was the Minister at the Zion Baptist Church in Washington D.C. ("Behind Her Eyes"). William Gibbons was buried at Charlottesville's Oakwood Cemetery; Isabella Gibbons is believed to be buried in an unmarked grave near her husband (accordingly, Isabella is not listed as a person buried at Oakwood; William's gravestone is below):</p><p>William D.
