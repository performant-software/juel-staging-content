<p>JUEL ID/KEY:&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=48327" target="_blank" title="P48327">P48327</a></p><p>"J. P. Hawkins," a photograph by Rufus W. Holsinger taken on August 24, 1917.<br /><br />The UVA Library Catalogue of the Holsinger collection states the following: "African American Family <a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1308" title="Belle Hawkins">Belle Hawkins</a>&nbsp;(<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=48328" target="_blank" title="P48328">P48328</a>), seated front row left hand; her husband, Lewis Hawkins, standing back row, second from left hand."&nbsp;Additional photos of&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1308" title="Belle Hawkins">Belle Hawkins</a>&nbsp;are also in the Holsinger catalogue.</p><p>It also appears that <a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1214" title="Mr. and Mrs. Cain Hawkins">Cain Hawkins</a>&nbsp;(photographed by Holsinger in a separate portrait) is in the photograph below, seated front row, second from left. <a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1505" title="Hawkins, T. C.">T. C. Hawkins</a> (photographed by Holsinger in a separate portrait) may be in the portrait below (both to be verified). &nbsp;</p><p>J. P. Hawkins may be James P. Hawkins, Jr., "mgr [manager] King Lumber Co h Frys Spring rd" (p100,&nbsp;<em>Charlottesville City Directory 1914-15</em>). In 1909, the warehouse for King Lumber Company was built (south side of Preston Avenue). The building is on the <a href="https://www.dhr.virginia.gov/historic-registers/104-0247/" target="_blank" title="Virginia Historic Register">Virginia Historic Register</a>.</p><p>Other businesses/owners photographed by Holsinger include&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/2164" target="_blank" title="Acme Shaving Parlor Ad">Acme Shaving Parlor</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1186" target="_blank" title="William F. and Charles H. Brown">William F. and Charles H. Brown</a>&nbsp;(Brown Barber Shop),&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1190" target="_blank" title="Buckner, A. T.">A. T. Buckner</a>&nbsp;(and son George F. Buckner, grocers),&nbsp;<a href="https://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1127" target="_blank" title="George Carr's Second Hand Clothing">George Carr's Second Hand Clothing</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1129" target="_blank" title="Dr. George R. Ferguson">Dr. George F. Ferguson</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1216" target="_blank" title="Dr. Johnson">Dr. George R. Johnson</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1307" target="_blank" title="Mr. J.P. Hawkins">Mr. J. P. Hawkins</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1504" target="_blank" title="W. G. Layne">W. G. Layne</a>&nbsp;(Layne and Whitlock, Wheelright/Blacksmith),&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/2180" title="J.F. Taylor">J.F. Taylor</a>, and&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1530" target="_blank" title="D.J. Ward">D. J. Ward</a>.&nbsp;See "<a href="https://americanhistory.si.edu/blog/freedoms-tally-african-american-business-jim-crow-south" target="_blank" title="&quot;Freedman's Tally&quot;">Freedman's Tally</a>" for more information about African Americans operating businesses in the Jim Crow Era.</p><p>Additional information about J.
