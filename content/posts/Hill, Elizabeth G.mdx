Elizabeth Govan Hill ([P47935](/en/people/ba24f9a0-9abe-4f3c-832b-3c734d35b5c9 "P47935")) (signed E.G. Hill), slaveowner, is known through her correspondence with John Staige Davis ([P43680](/en/people/3a14cfdd-ac55-448b-83dc-43261a120517 "P43680")). Several items in the John Staige Davis Papers mention Miss Hill (see links below for digitized transcripts). 

The "List of Miss EG Hill's Servants **Jan 13th 1862**," lists her as owner of 29 slaves total, female and male, ranging in age from one month to 60 years old.

The "Deed of Sale of Agnes to J.S. Davis," **August 14, 1862** documents Hill's sale of the slave [Agnes](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/864 "Agnes") (P47934) and her four children to Davis. Agnes's children are: [Randall](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/865 "Randall") (P47936), [Peggy](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/866 "Peggy") (P47937), [Jarratt](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/867 "Jarratt") (P47938), [infant boy](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/868 "Infant boy") ANON0060 (P47939).

The 24 servants recorded in the "List of Servants" are: [Henry](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/873 "----, HENRY") (P47941), [Randall](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/872 "----, RANDALL") (P47940), [Bennett](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/874 "----, BENNETT") (P47942), [Lucius](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/875 "----, LUCIUS") (P47943), [Carter](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/876 "----, CARTER") (P47944), [Susan](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/877 "----, SUSAN") (P47945), [Jarratt](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/878 "----, JARRATT") (P47964), [Elvira](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/879 "----, ELVIRA") (P47946), [Edmund](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/880 "----, EDMUND") (P47947), [Harriett](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/881 "----, HARRIETT") (P47948), [Richard](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/882 "----, RICHARD") (P47949), [Julia](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/883 "----, JULIA") (P47950), [John](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/884 "----, JOHN") (P47951), [Ann](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/885 "----, ANN") (P47952), [Aaron](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/886 "----, AARON") (P47953), [Lucy](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/887 "----, LUCY") (P47954), [May](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/888 "----, MAY") (P47955), [Robert](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/889 "----, ROBERT") (P47956), [John](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/890 "----, JOHN") (P47957), [Harry](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/891 "----, HARRY") (P47958), [Queen](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/892 "----, QUEEN") (P47959), [anon0061](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/869 "ANON0060") (P47960), [anon0062](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/870 "ANON0062") (P47961), [anon0063](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/871 "ANON0063") (P47962). 

Additional identifying information includes the Louisa County Birth Records for **1858**, which confirm Hill as the owner of Peggy ([P47937](/en/people/b9a61a97-789a-4bc2-b9ea-09fe395a8b3c "P47937")), one of the four children of Agnes ([P47934](/en/people/5dbfe1f4-30c0-49bb-9e49-e65f382468eb "P47934")) later sold to Davis. The entry states: Peggy, b. February 23, 1858. Colored, Slave, Female. Owner Elizabeth G. Hill, additional owner Dr. Jno. S. Burruss ([P44330](/en/people/8a73537a-e8ee-429a-9d99-62cc8053a23b "P44330")) and mother Agnes. The birth date of 1858 confirms Pegg's stated age of four in the 1862 Deed of Sale document to Davis. 

Elizabeth Hill and her servants are also mentioned in earlier correspondence between John Staige Davis and H. B. Bibb, dated **January 13, 1860** and **January 17, 1860**. On January 13th, Davis responds on behalf of him and E. G. Hill, or "Betty," regarding employment arrangements for Agnes and her children (to be close to her unnamed husband). 

On January 17th, Davis writes to Bibb ([P47976](/en/people/3424a075-e6f6-477b-84a4-cc19719a0482 "P47976")) about his finding a "suitable home" for Agnes. It appears from the letter that Hill and Davis both have some kind of ownership position over Agnes, and that Bibb was to procure employment for her. 

In Davis's letter to James Crawford (**December 17, 1861**), Davis writes, "I hope you will not consider Miss Hill too urgent in applying again for the hires of her servants, Harry & John, now due nearly twelve months. She is in much need of the money."
