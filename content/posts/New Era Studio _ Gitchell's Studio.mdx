<p><span style="font-size: small;"><strong>JUEL ID/KEY:&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_cb?cb=CB1144" title="CB1144">CB1144</a><br /></strong></span></p><p><span style="font-size: small;">The New Era Studio (also referred to as Gitchell's Studio) was a photography studio located at <strong>403 E Main Street</strong>, Charlottesville. The exact years of its operation are not yet known; it was in operation during 1914-15, as evident from information in the Charlottesville City Directory. Note that earlier, in 1905,<span>&nbsp;</span><strong><a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1164" title="Seabrook Bros. Advertisement, 1905-6">Seabrook Bros.</a>&nbsp;</strong>(<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_cb?cb=CB1143" title="CB1143">CB1143</a>) Studio was also located at 403 E. Main Street, Charlottesville.</span></p><p><span style="font-size: small;">This photograph by Holsinger, interestingly enough, shows the building signage for the New Era Studio.
