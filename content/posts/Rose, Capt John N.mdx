John N. Rose, Sr. was a hotelkeeper in the Academical Village, running Hotel F (<span id="docs-internal-guid-9e5c416d-3df0-b29a-44c8-fe62c92d0749"><a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_place?place=8437#m1" title="PL8437">PL8437</a>) from 1829-1833 and Hotel D (<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_place?place=8436" title="PL8436">PL8436</a>) from July 1833-4.&nbsp;<br /><br /></span></p><p>As hotelkeeper, Capt. Rose would have been in charge of the several slaves needed for running the boarding house and dining room. Two servants in particular that are mentioned are&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/797" title="----, TOM">Tom</a>&nbsp;(P47184) and&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/825" title="----, BEN">Ben</a>&nbsp;(P47778).&nbsp;<br /><br /></p><p>See also an 1847&nbsp;<a href="http://library.uncg.edu/slavery/petitions/pDetailsNew.aspx?pID=7374" target="_blank" title="Court Petition">court petition</a>&nbsp;involving a slave's petition for freedom (occupation - dining room servant) from Erasmus T.
