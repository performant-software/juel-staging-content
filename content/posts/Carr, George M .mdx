<p>JUEL ID/KEY:&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=48188" target="_blank" title="P48188">P48188</a><strong><br /></strong></p><p><span size="2">"George M. Carr," a photograph by Rufus W. Holsinger taken on November 14, 1916.</span></p><p><span size="2">George M. Carr, African-American businessman, 1900's. Photographed by Holsinger in front of his store on November 16, 1914. <br /></span></p><p><span size="2">The Charlottesville City Directory, 1914-15, lists the store as such: "Geo. M. Carr &amp; Co., Second Hand Clothing" (<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_place?place=9568" title="PL9568">PL9568</a>), located at 269 West Main Street (Ridge Street the nearby cross-street). Carr himself is listed as "Carr George M, clothier h[ome] 125 4th N W" (p.57).</span></p><p><span size="2">Perhaps related to Hugh Carr. Also, the George Carr photographed may be the "George Minor Carr" listed in the J. F. Bell Funeral Home records (b. June 22, 1882, d. September 3, 1933).&nbsp;For more information about the Carr/Greer family, visit the&nbsp;<a href="https://ivycreekfoundation.org/carr-greer-family-residence" target="_blank" title="Ivy Creek Foundation Carr/Greer Family Residence Web Site">Ivy Creek Foundation Carr/Greer Family Residence</a>&nbsp;web site.&nbsp;</span></p><p><span size="2">This photograph is especially important in showing one of several businesses run by African Americans in this time period. The images, along with the City Directories, tell us the location of the businesses, where the proprietors lived, their potential customers, the neighborhoods or business districts, and so on, helping us to better understand the actual lived environment of Charlottesville and surrounding counties in the Jim-Crow era. See "<a href="https://americanhistory.si.edu/blog/freedoms-tally-african-american-business-jim-crow-south" target="_blank" title="&quot;Freedman's Tally&quot;">Freedman's Tally</a>" for more information about African Americans operating businesses in the Jim Crow Era.</span></p><p>Other businesses/owners photographed by Holsinger include&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/2164" target="_blank" title="Acme Shaving Parlor Ad">Acme Shaving Parlor</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1186" target="_blank" title="William F. and Charles H. Brown">William F. and Charles H. Brown</a>&nbsp;(Brown Barber Shop),&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1190" target="_blank" title="Buckner, A. T.">A. T. Buckner</a>&nbsp;(and son George F.
