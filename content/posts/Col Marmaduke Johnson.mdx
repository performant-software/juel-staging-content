	Col. Marmaduke Johnson
author: >
	Philip Rubio (pmr2jq)
---
<p>Col. Marmaduke Johnson (1826-1871) served on the Board of Visitors from 1865-1871. Born in Richmond, he received a law degree from the University of Richmond and became the commonwealth&rsquo;s attorney in 1852. He began military service in 1862, forming and commanding the Richmond Light Artillery Battery and leading them into battle at Antietam. He rose to the rank of Lieutenant Colonel in 1865 at the end of the war. Before the war, Johnson was a delegate to the Virginia Secession Convention of 1861, fighting against secession for Richmond. He died in Richmond in 1871.</p>
