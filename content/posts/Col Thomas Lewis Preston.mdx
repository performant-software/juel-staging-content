<p>Col. Thomas Lewis Preston (1812-1903), a member of the Board of Visitors from 1849-1870, studied law at Virginia from 1830-1833 before serving in the Virginia General Assembly and sitting on several university boards. Preston rose to the rank of decommissioned general in the Civil War for his efforts at Bull Run and recieved praise from Stonewall Jackson and Confederate Secretary of War Judah Benjamin. Preston became UVA&#39;s Rector from 1864-1865 and kept the school intact during Union occupation. He was known for his oratory skills and charisma through the entirety of the Civil War and Reconstruction before dying in 1903. He was also known for his &ldquo;historical sketches&rdquo; about southwest Virginia.&nbsp;</p>
