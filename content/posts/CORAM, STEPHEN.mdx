<p><strong>JUEL ID/KEY:&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=47365" title="P47365">P47365</a></strong></p><p>Stephen Coram was a freed man of colour who lived in the area surrounding the university. He is mentioned in the&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/minishowcase2/" target="_blank" title="John Hartwell Cocke Papers">John Hartwell Cocke papers</a>&nbsp;in a discussion between&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/648" title="JOHN HARTWELL COCKE">John Hartwell Cocke</a>&nbsp;(<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=43616" title="P43616">P43616</a>) and Alexander Garrett about the purchase of property once owned by <a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/717" title="Robert Battles">Robert Battles</a>&nbsp;(<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=47365" title="P47365">P47363</a>). Garrett writes, "<span>I fear you are too late in urging Nelson to make the purchase of&nbsp;</span><span>the mulattos who hold the remainder of Battles’s original lot, I know you&nbsp;</span><span>did desire Nelson to make the purchase. . . yet I understood&nbsp;</span><span>from Dinsmore on yesterday, that he had purchased Coram out; (Coram you&nbsp;</span><span>will recollect holds one acre at the upper end of the lot opposite&nbsp;</span><span>Dinsmore’s gate) whether it is on his own account or for you I cannot&nbsp;</span><span>positively say . . ."</span>&nbsp;(c. 1825).&nbsp;</p><p>&nbsp;</p><p>&nbsp;</p><p><strong>PRIMARY SOURCE INFORMATION</strong>&nbsp;</p><p><strong>1.
