<p>JUEL ID/KEY:&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=48261" target="_blank" title="P48261">P48261</a></p><p><span size="2">Untitled (Dr. Johnson), photograph taken by Rufus W. Holsinger on April 29, 1912.</span></p><p>Comments from Family Photo Day, 9 March 2019:&nbsp;</p><p>"This is Dr. Johnson. He had an office on 4th St in a building that's still standing - it's a house for the homeless now. Two black doctors lived next door to each other - the other doctor's name was Dr. Jackson - he was a dentist. Dr. Johnson would come around to your house - he was a family practitioner."&nbsp;- Mr. Melvin Flannagan Jr., participant at Family Photo Day.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>Dr. George R. Johnson was listed in the Charlottesville City Directory, 1914-15 as a doctor working from an office located at 115 4th N W&nbsp;(<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_place?place=9573" title="PL9573">PL9573</a>) (p. 209). Other businesses/owners photographed by Holsinger include&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/2164" target="_blank" title="Acme Shaving Parlor Ad">Acme Shaving Parlor</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1186" target="_blank" title="William F. and Charles H. Brown">William F. and Charles H. Brown</a>&nbsp;(Brown Barber Shop),&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1190" target="_blank" title="Buckner, A. T.">A. T. Buckner</a>&nbsp;(and son George F. Buckner, grocers),&nbsp;<a href="https://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1127" target="_blank" title="George Carr's Second Hand Clothing">George Carr's Second Hand Clothing</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1129" target="_blank" title="Dr. George R. Ferguson">Dr. George F. Ferguson</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1216" target="_blank" title="Dr. Johnson">Dr. George R. Johnson</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1307" target="_blank" title="Mr. J.P. Hawkins">Mr. J. P.
