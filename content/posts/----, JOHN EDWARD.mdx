John Edward, male (last name unknown); owned by John Douglass ([P47156](/en/people/e48f7347-49b0-456c-a6de-2cf690e82c0a "P47156")) of Albemarle County. He is mentioned ten times in the Proctor's Daybook, from 1822 to 1827:   
  

*   September 25, 1822: "To John Douglass for hire of John this present year - $65.00."
*   November 26, 1822: "Bills Payable for the Proctor's bonds for the hire of Negroes To John Douglass for the hire of John $65."
*   February 24, 1823: "To Douglass for the hire of John Edwards," at $70.00 for one year.
*   February 27, 1823: "Sundries draft to A. S. Brockenbrough ([P43627](/en/people/3cf66e55-3c68-473d-b75d-566f5acb7b79 "P43627")) for this sum to Douglass for hire of John Ed. $60.00."
*   January 1, 1824: "Paid John Douglass for hire of John Edward $60.00."
*   January 4, 1825: "Labour acct. draft to Bills Payable to John Douglass for hire of John Ed. $60.00"
*   January 8, 1826: "Sundries with Bills Payable Labour acct. draft of J. Douglass John Edw. $.50 Bonded."
*   January 1, 1827: "\[Memorandum\] Labour acct. draft Bills Payable for hire of the following labourers this year - John Edward of J. Douglass $50.00"
