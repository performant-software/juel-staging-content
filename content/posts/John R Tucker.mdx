title: John R. Tucker

<p>John Randolph Tucker (1823-1897) graduated Virginia with an undergraduate degree in mathematics before attending Richmond Academy and later returned to Virginia for law school where he graduated in 1844. Tucker then opened a private law firm in his hometown of Winchester, VA before becoming attorney general of Virginia from 1857-1865. He served on the Board of Visitors from 1855-1859 and finished his political career as a member of the US House of Representatives from 1875-1887. He then went to teach law at Washington and Lee and served as President of the American Bar Association in 1894 before he died in 1897. In Congress, he served on the Ways and Means Committee with a particular focus on tariffs, and was praised for his oratory skills and patriotism. As a professor of law, he specialized in equity and public law, becoming known for his expertise on equity, international law, corporate and insurance law, and more.</p>
