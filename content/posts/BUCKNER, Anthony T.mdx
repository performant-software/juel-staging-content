	BUCKNER, Anthony T.

<p><span>JUEL ID/KEY:&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=48182" target="_blank" title="P48182">P48182</a></span></p><p>Anthony T. Buckner was a well-known Charlottesville merchant and former enslaved servant of the Fife family. He was born in 1846, and died December 24, 1923; he is buried (along with family) at the Daughters of Zion Cemetery, Charlottesville.</p><p>He is photographed by R. W. Holsinger on August 12, 1918 (<a href="http://hero.village.virginia.edu/~rwb3y/hol-uv/?manifests=manifests-hol.json#?c=0&amp;m=0&amp;s=0&amp;cv=338&amp;xywh=-2571%2C-222%2C7914%2C4427" title="Buckner, Anthony T.">photo ID: X06569A</a>) (likely mislabelled as, "A. <strong>J.</strong>&nbsp;Buckner"). A young girl with him in the portrait is likely his granddaughter, Eileen Woods Buckner, born 3/11/1909&nbsp;(given the date of the photograph).</p><div class="page" title="Page 104"><div class="layoutArea"><div class="column"><p><span>Anthony had been the body servant of James Fife throughout the Civil War, and later became a grocer running a store at 904 West Main for nearly forty years (Blue Ribbon Commission p.26-7). His wife was Mary Churchman Buckner (1859-1934).&nbsp;</span></p><p>Other businesses/owners photographed by Holsinger include&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/2164" target="_blank" title="Acme Shaving Parlor Ad">Acme Shaving Parlor</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1186" target="_blank" title="William F. and Charles H. Brown">William F. and Charles H. Brown</a>&nbsp;(Brown Barber Shop),&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1190" target="_blank" title="Buckner, A. T.">A. T. Buckner</a>&nbsp;(and son George F. Buckner, grocers),&nbsp;<a href="https://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1127" target="_blank" title="George Carr's Second Hand Clothing">George Carr's Second Hand Clothing</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1129" target="_blank" title="Dr. George R. Ferguson">Dr. George F. Ferguson</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1216" target="_blank" title="Dr. Johnson">Dr. George R. Johnson</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1307" target="_blank" title="Mr. J.P. Hawkins">Mr. J. P. Hawkins</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1504" target="_blank" title="W. G. Layne">W. G. Layne</a>&nbsp;(Layne and Whitlock, Wheelright/Blacksmith),&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/2180" title="J.F. Taylor">J.F. Taylor</a>, and&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1530" target="_blank" title="D.J. Ward">D. J. Ward</a>.&nbsp;See "<a href="https://americanhistory.si.edu/blog/freedoms-tally-african-american-business-jim-crow-south" target="_blank" title="&quot;Freedman's Tally&quot;">Freedman's Tally</a>" for more information about African Americans operating businesses in the Jim Crow Era.</p><p><span>The Charlottesville 1895 "People of Property" record (tax asessments of taxable property - land acreage and building values) listed owners by "colored" or "white"; Buckner in this year owned lot and buildings together valued at 1000$ and more ("People of Property").<br /></span></p><p><span>In 1902, A. T. Buckner was registered as a qualified voter in Charlottesville's Fourth Ward for the November 1902 election (the first one following the change in Virginia's Constitution, which had disenfranchised many African Americans).&nbsp;</span>The below article from the Daily Progress notes,&nbsp;“The Board of Registrars for the fourth ward of the city was comprised of Messrs. Thomas S. Jones, chairman, Roy K. Flannagan and Water Dinwiddie. The board registered during its sitting the following qualified voters":&nbsp;</p><p>"J. Allen, Robt. Bullock,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1190" title="Buckner, A. T."><strong>A. T. Buckner</strong></a>, G. W. Brown, Henry Blue, J. W. Burns, Charles Burley, R. T. Brown, J. L. Coles, Wm. E. Catlett, Wm. Dickerson, Robert Flood, Charles Fuller, C. D. Goodloe, Charles Goodloe, [C] S. Hooker, Elder Harris, Albert Hooker, Hudson Jenkins, Robert Keller, G. W. Lawson, W. O. Lewis,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1533#overlay-context=node/996" title="Moon, John E."><strong>J. E. Moon</strong></a>, J. P. Michie, Thos. Preston, R. C. Quarles, Warner Rivers, Albert Southall,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1447" title="Tonsler, B. E."><strong>B. E. Tonsler</strong></a>, Lee Watson, Allen Watson and P. Y. Wyatt."&nbsp;</p><p><img src="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/sites/default/files/media/images/Screen%20Shot%202019-11-19%20at%2011.32.10%20AM.png" alt="List of Colored Voters, 1902" title="List of Colored Voters, 1902" width="579" height="456" /></p><p></p><p></p><p><span>Anthony T. Buckner had a son, George W. Buckner (father to Eileen). His son George W. Buckner (1887-1921) was also a well-known individual. A 1914-15 listing in the Charlottesville City Directory notes that George continued the grocery store, listing him as "grocer, 904 West Main h[ome]" living at "322 6th S W."&nbsp;He was the Executive Secretary of the St. Louis Urban League.</span></p><p><span>Anthony, George Buckner's wife Geneva Tonsler Buckner and daughter Eileen, and Geneva's parents (Horace and Pocahantas Tonsler) are all buried near one another at the Daughters of Zion Cemetery (Blue Ribbon Commission p.27). &nbsp;</span></p><p><span>Update: the Burial List (May 22, 2019) notes the following Buckner family members buried at the&nbsp;Daughters of Zion Cemetery: Annie, Anthony T., Eileen, Geneva J., Hattie M., James A., John T., Louisa E., Robert, and Susie J.</span></p><p><em>The Charlottesville Messenger</em>, edited and published by John G. Shelton, principal of the Albemarle Training School, was Charlottesville’s African American newspaper from 1910 until about 1927.
