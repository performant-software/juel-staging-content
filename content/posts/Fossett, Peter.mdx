**JUEL ID/KEY:** [P48183](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=48183 "P48183")

Rev. Peter Fossett, formerly enslaved at Monticello, was said to have taught Cynthia Bullock's son, Burkley, how to read (according to family history).  

Below: The Richmond Dispatch, Richmond Virginia, January 25, 1885

![](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/sites/default/files/media/images/26992061_558716827814703_7271444123313210875_n.jpg "Fossett/Bullock Article")  

  
An account of Fossett can be seen here in the following article ("Was Jefferson's Slave," reprinted in Charlottesville's Daily Progress May 25, 1900):   
![Peter Fossett Newspaper Article](http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/sites/default/files/media/images/Screen%20Shot%202020-04-27%20at%201.34.10%20PM.png "Peter Fossett Newspaper Article")

The Monticello web site has a detailed account of [Peter Fossett](https://www.monticello.org/site/research-and-collections/peter-fossett?fbclid=IwAR3AH2WWlOBbRIozcPWUL-5wyjpEnMVwCqFmk_qLhlD0b0FWm-mzAlzkJe4 "Peter Fossett Newspaper Article"), including photographs.
