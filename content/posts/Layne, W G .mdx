<p>JUEL ID/KEY:&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=48670" target="_blank" title="P48670">P48670</a></p><p>"W. G. Layne," a photograph by Rufus W. Holsinger taken on June 1, 1917.</p><p>An African-American owned Blacksmith and Wheelwright company, called "Layne &amp; Whitlock," was located at "S. 3rd nr Water S E." W. <em>G.</em> Layne may possibly be a typo for William <em>J.</em> Layne, blacksmith, the Layne who owned this company along with W. C. Whitlock (Charlottesville City Directory 1914-15, p. 123, 204). A William J. Layne lived at 125 4th St. N W (p. 125). Mr. Whitlock of the same company was William C. Whitlock.&nbsp;</p><p>Other businesses/owners photographed by Holsinger include&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/2164" target="_blank" title="Acme Shaving Parlor Ad">Acme Shaving Parlor</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1186" target="_blank" title="William F. and Charles H. Buckner</a>&nbsp;(and son George F. Buckner, grocers),&nbsp;<a href="https://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1127" target="_blank" title="George Carr's Second Hand Clothing">George Carr's Second Hand Clothing</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1129" target="_blank" title="Dr. George R.
