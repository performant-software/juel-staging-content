<p><span>JUEL ID/KEY:&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=48259" target="_blank" title="P48259">P48259</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=48671" target="_blank" title="P48671">P48671</a><strong><strong><a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=48259" target="_blank" title="P48259"><br /></a></strong></strong></span><br />"Mr. and Mrs. Cain (Alice Wood) Hawkins," a photograph by Rufus W. Holsinger taken on August 20, 1915.<br /><br />Cain (<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=48259" target="_blank" title="P48259">P48259</a>)&nbsp;and his wife, Alice Wood Hawkins (<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/public_person?person=48671" target="_blank" title="P48671">P48671</a>), are&nbsp;listed in the J. F. Bell Funeral Home database: Cain Hawkins, born August 9, 1862 in Millington, Virginia to parents Frank and Elizabeth Hawkins (mother's maiden name Wheeler); died February 4, 1939 in Charlottesville, Virginia and buried at White Hall.</p><p>The entry for Alice states: Alice Wood Hawkins, born September 1, 1867 in Albemarle City, Virginia to parents B. F. and Cinthia Wood (mother's maiden name Speck); died December 29, 1948 in Charlottesville, Virginia and buried at White Hall and buried at White Hall.&nbsp;</p><p>Comments from Family Photo Day, 9 March 2019, by Ms. Dede Smith:</p><p><span>"Photo is: Cain Hawkins &amp; Alice Wood Hawkins. Cain's parents are Frank Hawkins and Elizabeth (enslaved) Harris or Wheeler [both surnames seen in documents]. </span></p><p><span>[location]: [M]illington by Whitehall, Whitehall / Brown's Gap.</span></p><p><span>Cain's sister, Texie Mae Hawkins married Hugh Carr of union Ridge - River View Farm (now Ivy Creek Natural Area).&nbsp;</span></p><p><span>Hugh &amp; Texie Mae have 6 children, the eldest Mary Carr Greer for whom Greer Elementary School (Charlottesville) is named. The Carrs and 2nd gen. Greers would visit Whitehall regularly for family visits. Cain &amp; Texie Mae were very close. Cain &amp; Alice had 13 children.</span></p><p><span>Frank &amp; Elizabeth had 7 children:<br />- Cain (in Holsinger photo), Charlotte, Texie Mae, Leslie, Willie Ann, Elizabeth (called Aunt Frankie) &amp; Emma Louisa (youngest)</span></p><p><span>Emma Louisa (youngest) married Bezeal ("Bee") White. Bee White's brother, John Flournoy White married Sarah Lewis and had 5 children, the oldest of whom was Randolph White of the Tribune.&nbsp;</span></p><p><span>Marcha Howard (living) (Union Zion Church, Charlottesville) is Emma &amp; Bee's great-great-granddaughter."</span></p><p>Additional Information about Cain and the Hawkins family:<br />Cain was born into slavery (his mother Elizabeth Wheeler Hawkins owned by the Gillums in Millington, off Garth Road, then sold to the Harrises).&nbsp;</p><p>Texie Mae Hawkins (Cain's sister) was the second wife of Hugh Carr; they had 6 daughters and 1 son (Mary Louise, Fannie, Emma, Peachie, Hazel, Virginia, and Marshall). All attended the Union Ridge Graded School ("The Life &amp; Legacy of Hugh Carr").</p><p>*To be determined: related to&nbsp;<a href="http://juel.iath.virginia.edu/node/1307" title="J. P. Hawkins">J.
